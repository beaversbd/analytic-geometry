Homework for Vector Projection

Complete these for your own practice. They will not be collected.

  1. Find the projection \(\overrightarrow{w}\) and perpendicular projection \(\overrightarrow{w_\perp}\) of \(\overrightarrow{u}\) onto \(\overrightarrow{v}\):

    1. \(\overrightarrow{u}=\langle 1,2\rangle\) and \(\overrightarrow{v}=\langle 2,5\rangle\)

      \(\overrightarrow{w}=\frac{\overrightarrow{u}\cdot\overrightarrow{v}}{\lVert\overrightarrow{v}\rVert^2}\;\overrightarrow{v}=\frac{\langle 1,2\rangle\cdot\langle 2,5\rangle}{\lVert\langle 2,5\rangle\rVert^2}\langle 2,5\rangle=\frac{12}{(\sqrt{29})^2}\langle 2,5\rangle=\langle\frac{24}{29},\frac{60}{29}\rangle\) and
      \(\overrightarrow{w_\perp}=\overrightarrow{u}-\overrightarrow{w}=\langle 1,2\rangle-\langle\frac{24}{29},\frac{60}{29}\rangle=\langle\frac{5}{29},-\frac{2}{29}\rangle.\)

    2. \(\overrightarrow{u}=\langle -1,2\rangle\) and \(\overrightarrow{v}=\langle 1,4 \rangle\)

      \(\overrightarrow{w}=\frac{\overrightarrow{u}\cdot\overrightarrow{v}}{\lVert\overrightarrow{v}\rVert^2}\;\overrightarrow{v}=\frac{\langle -1,2\rangle\cdot\langle 1,4\rangle}{\lVert\langle 1,4\rangle\rVert^2}\langle 1,4\rangle=\frac{7}{(\sqrt{17})^2}\langle 1,4\rangle=\langle\frac{7}{17},\frac{28}{17}\rangle\) and
      \(\overrightarrow{w_\perp}=\overrightarrow{u}-\overrightarrow{w}=\langle -1,2\rangle-\langle\frac{7}{17},\frac{28}{17}\rangle=\langle-\frac{24}{17},\frac{6}{17}\rangle.\)

    3. \(\overrightarrow{u}=\langle -1,-1\rangle\) and \(\overrightarrow{v}=\langle 4,-1\rangle\)

      \(\overrightarrow{w}=\frac{\overrightarrow{u}\cdot\overrightarrow{v}}{\lVert\overrightarrow{v}\rVert^2}\;\overrightarrow{v}=\frac{\langle -1,-1\rangle\cdot\langle 4,-1\rangle}{\lVert\langle 4,-1\rangle\rVert^2}\langle 4,-1\rangle=\frac{-3}{(\sqrt{17})^2}\langle 4,-1\rangle=\langle-\frac{12}{17},\frac{3}{17}\rangle\) and
      \(\overrightarrow{w_\perp}=\overrightarrow{u}-\overrightarrow{w}=\langle -1,-1\rangle-\langle-\frac{12}{17},\frac{3}{17}\rangle=\langle\frac{29}{17},-\frac{20}{17}\rangle.\)
