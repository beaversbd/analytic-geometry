Homework Solutions - Rotation Equations

1a. \(xy=4; \alpha=45^\circ\)

Since \(\alpha=45^\circ,\) we get the rotation equations \(x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\) and \(y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'.\)

\[ \begin{align} xy &= 4 \\ \left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right)\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right) &= 4 \\ \frac{1}{2}(x')^2+\frac{1}{2}x'y'-\frac{1}{2}x'y'-\frac{1}{2}(y')^2 &= 4 \\ \frac{1}{2}(x')^2- \frac{1}{2}(y')^2 &= 4 \\ \frac{(x')^2}{8}-\frac{(y')^2}{8} &= 1. \end{align} \]

This is a hyperbola with horizontal transverse axis:

1b. \(x^2-2xy+y^2+x+y=0; \alpha=45^\circ\)

Since \(\alpha=45^\circ,\) we get the rotation equations \(x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\) and \(y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'.\)

\[ \begin{align} x^2-2xy+y^2+x+y &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right)^2}-\textcolor{purple}{2\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right)\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right)}\\ +\textcolor{blue}{\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right)^2}+\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right)+\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'\right) &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{\left(\frac{1}{2}(x')^2-\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{1}{2}(y')^2\right)}-\textcolor{purple}{2\left(\frac{1}{2}(x')^2+\frac{1}{2}x'y'-\frac{1}{2}x'y'-\frac{1}{2}(y')^2\right)}\\ +\textcolor{blue}{\left(\frac{1}{2}(x')^2+\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{1}{2}(y')^2\right)}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y'+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}y' &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{\left(\frac{1}{2}(x')^2-\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{1}{2}(y')^2\right)}+\textcolor{purple}{\left(-\frac{2}{2}(x')^2-\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{2}{2}(y')^2\right)}\\ +\textcolor{blue}{\left(\frac{1}{2}(x')^2+\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{1}{2}(y')^2\right)}+\frac{2}{\sqrt{2}}x' &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{\left(\frac{1}{2}(x')^2-\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{1}{2}(y')^2\right)}+\textcolor{purple}{\left(-\frac{2}{2}(x')^2+\frac{2}{2}(y')^2\right)}+\textcolor{blue}{\left(\frac{1}{2}(x')^2+\frac{2}{2}x'y'+\frac{1}{2}(y')^2\right)}+\frac{2}{\sqrt{2}}x' &= 0 \\ \left(\textcolor{red}{\frac{1}{2}(x')^2}+\textcolor{purple}{-\frac{2}{2}(x')^2}+\textcolor{blue}{\frac{1}{2}(x')^2}\right)+\left(\textcolor{red}{-\frac{2}{2}x'y'}+\textcolor{blue}{\frac{2}{2}x'y'}\right)+\left(\textcolor{red}{\frac{1}{2}(y')^2}+\textcolor{purple}{\frac{2}{2}(y')^2}+\textcolor{blue}{\frac{1}{2}(y')^2}\right)+\frac{2}{\sqrt{2}}x' &= 0 \\ 2(y')^2+\frac{2}{\sqrt{2}}x' &= 0 \\ (y')^2+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x' &= 0 \\ (y')^2 &= -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}x'. \end{align} \]

This is a leftward opening parabola with vertex \((x',y')=(0,0)\) and \(c=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}\) (becuase \(4c=\sqrt{2}\)):

1c. \(31x^2+10\sqrt{3}xy+21y^2-144=0; \alpha=30^\circ\)

Since \(\alpha=30^\circ,\) we get the rotation equations \(x=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}x'-\frac{1}{2}y'\) and \(y=\frac{1}{2}x'+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}y'.\)

\[ \begin{align} 31x^2+10\sqrt{3}xy+21y^2-144 &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{31\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}x'-\frac{1}{2}y'\right)^2}+\textcolor{purple}{10\sqrt{3}\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}x'-\frac{1}{2}y'\right)\left(\frac{1}{2}x'+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}y'\right)}+\textcolor{blue}{21\left(\frac{1}{2}x'+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}y'\right)^2}-144 &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{31\left(\frac{3}{4}(x')^2-\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'+\frac{1}{4}(y')^2\right)^2}+\textcolor{purple}{10\sqrt{3}\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(x')^2+\frac{3}{4}x'y'-\frac{1}{4}x'y'-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(y')^2\right)}\\ +\textcolor{blue}{21\left(\frac{1}{4}(x')^2+\frac{2\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'+\frac{3}{4}(y')^2\right)} &= 144 \\ \textcolor{red}{\frac{93}{4}(x')^2-\frac{62\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'+\frac{31}{4}(y')}+\textcolor{purple}{\frac{30}{4}(x')^2+\frac{30\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'-\frac{10\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'-\frac{30}{4}(y')^2}\\ +\textcolor{blue}{\frac{21}{4}(x')^2+\frac{42\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'+\frac{63}{4}(y')} &= 144 \\ \left(\textcolor{red}{\frac{93}{4}(x')^2}+\textcolor{purple}{\frac{30}{4}(x')^2}+\textcolor{blue}{\frac{21}{2}(x')^2}\right)+\left(\textcolor{red}{-\frac{62\sqrt{3}}{2}x'y'}+\textcolor{purple}{\frac{20\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'}+\textcolor{blue}{\frac{42\sqrt{3}}{4}x'y'}\right)+\left(\textcolor{red}{\frac{31}{4}(y')^2}+\textcolor{purple}{-\frac{30}{4}(y')^2}+\textcolor{blue}{\frac{63}{4}(y')^2}\right) &= 144 \\ \frac{144}{4}(x')^2+\frac{64}{4}(y')^2 &= 144 \\ 36(x')^2+16(y')^2 &= 144 \\ \frac{(x')^2}{4}+\frac{(y')^2}{9} &= 1. \end{align} \]

This is an ellipse whose major axis is vertical with vertex \((x',y')=(0,0),\) \(a=3,\) \(b=2,\) and \(c=\sqrt{5}\):

1d. \(8x^2+5xy-4y^2-4=0; \alpha=\tan^{-1}(\frac{1}{5})\)

Since \(\alpha=\tan^{-1}(\frac{1}{5}),\) we can draw a triangle to find \(\cos\alpha\) and \(\sin\alpha\):

Thus \(\cos\alpha=\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}\) and \(\sin\alpha=\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}.\) We get the rotation equations \(x=\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}y'\) and \(y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}x'+\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}y'.\)

\[ \begin{align} 8x^2+5xy-4y^2-4 &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{8\left(\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}y'\right)^2}+\textcolor{purple}{5\left(\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}y'\right)\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}x'+\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}y'\right)}-\textcolor{blue}{4\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}x'+\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}y'\right)^2}-4 &= 0 \\ \textcolor{red}{8\left(\frac{25}{26}(x')^2-\frac{10}{26}x'y'+\frac{1}{26}(y')^2\right)^2}+\textcolor{purple}{5\left(\frac{5}{26}(x')^2+\frac{25}{26}x'y'-\frac{1}{26}x'y'-\frac{5}{26}(y')^2\right)}\\ +\textcolor{blue}{-4\left(\frac{1}{26}(x')^2+\frac{10}{26}x'y'+\frac{25}{26}(y')^2\right)} &= 4 \\ \textcolor{red}{\frac{200}{26}(x')^2-\frac{80}{26}x'y'+\frac{8}{26}(y')}+\textcolor{purple}{\frac{25}{26}(x')^2+\frac{125}{26}x'y'-\frac{5}{26}x'y'-\frac{25}{26}(y')^2}\\ +\textcolor{blue}{-\frac{4}{26}(x')^2-\frac{40}{26}x'y'-\frac{100}{26}(y')} &= 4 \\ \left(\textcolor{red}{\frac{200}{26}(x')^2}+\textcolor{purple}{\frac{25}{26}(x')^2}+\textcolor{blue}{-\frac{4}{26}(x')^2}\right)+\left(\textcolor{red}{-\frac{80}{26}x'y'}+\textcolor{purple}{\frac{120}{26}x'y'}+\textcolor{blue}{-\frac{40}{26}x'y'}\right)+\left(\textcolor{red}{\frac{8}{26}(y')^2}+\textcolor{purple}{-\frac{25}{26}(y')^2}+\textcolor{blue}{-\frac{100}{26}(y')^2}\right) &= 4 \\ \frac{221}{26}(x')^2-\frac{117}{26}(y')^2 &= 4 \\ \frac{(x')^2}{8/17}-\frac{(y')^2}{8/9} &= 1. \end{align} \]

This is a hyperbola whose transverse axis is horizontal vertex \((x',y')=(0,0),\) \(a=\sqrt{8/17},\) \(b=\sqrt{8/9},\) and \(c=\sqrt{208/153}\):
