    1. \(x^2+4xy-2y^2-6=0\)

      In this equation, \(A=1,\) \(B=4,\) and \(C=-2.\) Thus \(\tan\alpha=\frac{(C-A)\pm\sqrt{(C-A)^2+B^2}}{B}=\frac{(-2-1)\pm\sqrt{(-2-1)^2+4^2}}{4}=\frac{-3\pm 5}{4}=\frac{2}{4}\text{ or }-\frac{8}{4}.\) We choose the positive option: \(\tan\alpha=\frac{1}{2}.\) Here is an illustration of \(\alpha\):

      Thus \(\cos\alpha=\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}\) and \(\sin\alpha=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}.\) Therefore our rotation equations are \(x=\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}y'\) and \(y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}x'+\frac{2}{\sqrt{5}}y'.\) After substitution, you should get a standard form equation \(\frac{(x')^2}{3}-\frac{(y')^2}{2}=1\) (hyperbola).

    2. \(2x^2-xy+2y^2-2=0\)

      In this equation, \(A=C,\) and so \(\alpha=45^\circ.\) Thus \(\cos\alpha=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\) and \(\sin\alpha=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}.\) Therefore our rotation equations are \(x=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}x'-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}y'\) and \(y=\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}x'+\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}y'.\) After substitution, you should get a standard form equation \(\frac{(x')^2}{4/3}+\frac{(y')^2}{4/5}=1\) (ellipse).

      Thus \(\cos\alpha=\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}\) and \(\sin\alpha=\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}.\) Therefore our rotation equations are \(x=\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}x'-\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}y'\) and \(y=\frac{1}{\sqrt{26}}x'+\frac{5}{\sqrt{26}}y'.\) After substitution, you should get a standard form equation \(\frac{(x'-1)^2}{4}-\frac{(y'+2)^2}{1}=1\) (hyperbola).
