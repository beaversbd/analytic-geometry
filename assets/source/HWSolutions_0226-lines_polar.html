  1. Convert the following rectangular equations of lines to polar equations.

    1. \(y=\frac{1}{2}x\)

      \(r\sin\theta=\frac{1}{2} r\cos\theta,\) or since this is a line through the pole/origin: \(\theta=\tan^{-1}(1/2)\) or \(\tan\theta=\frac{1}{2}\)

    2. \(2x+3y=6\)

      \(2r\cos\theta+3r\sin\theta=6,\) or solved for \(r\): \(r=\frac{6}{2\cos\theta+3\sin\theta}\)

    3. \(y=-3x+5\)

      \(r\sin\theta=-3r\cos\theta+5,\) or solved for \(r\): \(r=\frac{5}{\sin\theta+3\cos\theta}\)

    4. \(y=-x\)

      This is a line through the pole, whose tangent is \(-1\), so \(\theta=-45^\circ\) or \(\theta=135^\circ\) are correct answers. Another correct answer is \(r\sin\theta=-r\cos\theta\)

    5. \(\frac{x}{4}+\frac{y}{-3}=1\)

      \(\frac{r\cos\theta}{4}+\frac{r\sin\theta}{-3}=1,\) or solved for \(r\): \(r=\frac{-12}{-3\cos\theta+4\sin\theta}\)

  2. Convert the following polar equations of lines to rectangular equations. Find the slope and any intercepts.

    1. \(\theta = 45^\circ\)

      This is the line through the origin with inclination \(45^\circ\). So its slope is \(\tan(45^\circ)=1.\) Thus \(y=1x+0\) is the line (or just \(y=x\)). The point \((0,0)\) is both an \(x\)-intercept and \(y\)-intercept, and is the only intercept.

    2. \(r=2\sec\theta\)

      Thus \(r=\frac{2}{\cos\theta}.\) Thus \(r\cos\theta=2.\) Therefore \(x=2.\) There is no slope, and \((2,0)\), an \(x\)-intercept, is the only intercept.

    3. \(r=-3\csc\theta\)

      Thus \(r=\frac{-3}{\sin\theta}.\) Thus \(r\sin\theta=-3.\) Therefore \(y=-3.\) The slope is zero and \((0,-3)\), a \(y\)-intercept, is the only intercept.

    4. \(r=\frac{4}{3\cos\theta+2\sin\theta}\)

      Multiplying both sides to eliminate the fraction, we get \(r(3\cos\theta+2\sin\theta)=4.\) Thus \(3r\cos\theta + 2r\sin\theta=4.\) Therefore \(3x+2y=4.\) Solving for \(y\), we get \(y=-\frac{3}{2}x+2\). Thus the slope is \(-3/2\), the \(y\)-intercept is \((0,2)\), and the \(x\)-intercept is \((4/3,0).\)
